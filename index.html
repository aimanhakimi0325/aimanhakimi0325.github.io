Introduction

The University of Malaya (UM) campus, like many metropolitan places, experiences flooding issues, especially after heavy rains. This platform is focused on finding, assessing, and managing flood-prone areas on our campus to ensure safety, sustainability, and resilience to future water-related concerns.

What You Will Find Here:

  • Flood Hotspot Maps: Explore interactive maps that indicate flood-prone regions on the UM campus.
  • Data and Insights: Learn about the causes, frequency, and impact of floods in specific places.
  • Preventive Measures: Learn about current and upcoming flood mitigation activities to safeguard the campus community.
  • Resources and Reports: Access emergency preparedness information and contribute by reporting any new flood-prone regions or hazards.

By collaborating, we can create a safer and more sustainable environment for students, faculty, and visitors. Join us in taking preventive efforts to mitigate floods and strengthen the resilience of our cherished UM campus.

Stay informed and safe.

Methodology

1. Study Area Definition

We focused on the University of Malaya (UM) campus, covering key areas such as academic buildings, residential halls, pathways, and open spaces. These areas were selected due to their high usage and vulnerability to flooding during heavy rainfall.

2. Data Collection Methods

  • Surveys and Interviews: Engaged with students, staff, and campus maintenance teams to gather firsthand accounts of flood-prone areas.
  • Field Observations: Conducted on-site inspections during and after rainfall events to record water accumulation and drainage performance.
  • Geospatial Mapping: Used GPS tools and GIS software to map low-lying areas and water flow patterns.
  • Meteorological Data: Collected historical and real-time rainfall data from the Malaysian Meteorological Department to correlate flooding incidents with precipitation intensity.

3. Data Analysis

  • Hotspot Identification: Combined observational data with GIS mapping to pinpoint recurring flood zones.
  • Severity Assessment: Classified hotspots based on flood depth, frequency, and duration.
  • Causal Analysis: Investigated contributing factors like poor drainage, topography, or urban infrastructure issues.

4. Tools and Technologies Used

  • GIS software for mapping and spatial analysis.
  • Digital photography and video recording for documentation.
  • Rain gauges and water level sensors for quantitative measurements.

5. Validation

Our findings were cross-verified with campus maintenance records and stakeholder feedback to ensure accuracy and reliability.

6. Continuous Monitoring

This project is ongoing, with periodic updates to incorporate new data and address changing campus conditions. Users can contribute by reporting observations via the “Report a Flood” feature on this website.

Background History

Flooding at the University of Malaya (UM) campus is a re-occurring issue due to its position in Kuala Lumpur, a city that is frequently hit by flash floods during heavy rainfall. Flood episodes on campus have impacted academic and administrative activity, including the suspension of some classes and causing difficulties for people and cars accessing the campus during these events.

Unseasonably excessive rainfall, surpassing the capacity of drainage systems, and closeness to flood-prone areas are all major contributors to floods at UM. For example, torrential rainfall in October 2024 created significant interruptions, forcing students and staff to switch to online classes due to dangerous conditions.

The administration monitors such activities and offers regular updates to promote safety among the university community. Flood mitigation efforts involve regular monitoring and drainage infrastructure improvements.
